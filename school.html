128 Years of Legacy
Our Heritage

A Legacy That Shapes
Every Generation

The story began in 1874, when Fr. Bonjean started English classes for the children of the North Central Province. On 19 March 1898, Bishop Henri Joulain of Jaffna registered it as a public English school – named after St. Joseph's Church and open to boys of every faith. Today it stands as a Grade 1AB national school and one of the region's most respected institutions.

A National School of Distinction
One of Sri Lanka's premier national schools, recognized for consistent academic excellence at O/L and A/L examinations nationwide.
Academic Powerhouse
Home to dedicated teachers and world-class facilities that push every Josephian to reach their highest potential.
Alumni Making Global Impact
Our graduates serve as ministers, doctors, engineers, and leaders across Sri Lanka and the world.
Our Journey

128 Years in the
Making

From a handful of English classes to a leading national school — the milestones that shaped St. Joseph's College.

1874
The First Classes
Fr. Bonjean begins English classes in Anuradhapura — the first English education in the North Central Province.
1898
Officially Founded
On 19 March, Bishop Henri Joulain of Jaffna registers the school as a public English school, named after St. Joseph's Church.
1968
A Government School
Management passes from the Catholic Church to the state; J. G. Jayasuriya is appointed the college's first principal.
1972
National School Status
SJC becomes a Grade 1AB national school for boys — a status it proudly holds today.
Today
A Living Legacy
128 years on, more than 200 teachers guide thousands of Josephians in the shadow of Anuradhapura's ancient stupas.

Proud Traditions

The Battle of
the Legends

Every year SJC meets Anuradhapura Central College in the "Battle of the Legends" – an annual cricket and football encounter that fills the city with maroon and gold and bonds every Josephian, past and present.

Battle of the Legends
The annual cricket & football clash with Anuradhapura Central College – the highlight of the sporting calendar.
Prize Giving Day
The grandest ceremony of the year, celebrating excellence in academics, sports, arts, and leadership.
